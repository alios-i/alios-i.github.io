AURION & ZEPHYRA

Discover Aurion, a mesmerizing celestial body that captivates astronomers and space enthusiasts alike. With its unique geological formations and mysterious surface, Aurion represents the frontier of our cosmic exploration.

Nestled in the far reaches of its solar system, this moon challenges our understanding of planetary formation and offers a glimpse into the complex dynamics of celestial mechanics.

NEXAR-B

R☉: 1.3, Temperature ≈ 3600°K, Habitable Zone: 0.5 - 0.7 AU, Type: M1III (Red Giant), Age: ~5.2 billion years, Mass: ~0.8 M☉

Nearby Companion: The star is part of a binary system with its faint partner, NEXAR-A, located 1.8 light-years away.

Overview

Welcome to the detailed exploration of Alios I, a terrestrial planet in the Nexar-B star system. Explore the interactive map of this project at NEXAR-B System Map.

Star Characteristics

  • Star Type: Red, cooler, and fainter than the Sun.
  • Habitable Zone: Narrow and close to the star.

Formation & Orbital Characteristics

  • System Age: 5.22 billion years (older than our solar system).
  • Number of Planets: 3.
  • Number of Moons: 4-10.
  • Orbital Eccentricity: 0.2.
  • Semi-Major Axis: 0.6 AU.
  • Orbital Period: 169.6 days (0.4648 Earth years).
  • Position: Inner Solar System.

Planet Physical Characteristics

  • Type: Terrestrial.
  • Albedo: 36% (similar to Earth).
  • Surface Composition: Mixture of water and rock.

Geology

  • Cratering: Low visibility due to erosion.
  • Volcanism: Active, with a molten core.
  • Tectonics: Active, with a molten core.
  • Erosion: Water and wind processes.
  • Surface Age: Somewhat old, with indications of erosion and atmospheric conditions.

Interior & Magnetic Field

  • Radius: 10,183.93 km (1.6× Earth).
  • Mass: 1.48×10²⁵ kg (2.48× Earth).
  • Density: 3.47 g/cm³.
  • Gravity: 9.524 m/s² (slightly less than Earth).
  • Magnetic Field: Weak, due to high silicate content and limited conductive materials.
  • Composition Options:
    • Option 1: 90.3% Silicate Rock, 9.54% Iron/Nickel, trace gases.
    • Option 2: 89.6% Silicate Rock, 9.66% Iron/Nickel, trace gases and water.
  • Rotation Rate: 16 hours (fast).

Atmosphere & Temperature

  • Effective Temperature: 361 K (near boiling; water remains liquid).
  • Albedo Temperature: 326 K (similar to a hot summer day).
  • Atmospheric Temperature: 383 K (uninhabitable; water in vapor state).
  • Optical Depth: 0.6 (thick atmosphere).
  • Composition: Oxygen, Nitrogen, CO2.
  • Weather: Slow winds, distinct seasons, minimal day-night temperature differences.

Chemical Conditions

  • Spectrum Type: Absorption.
